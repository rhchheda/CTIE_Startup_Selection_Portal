The problem

Selection was split across inboxes, attachments, and memory.

Scattered

Applications arrived as attachments in a dozen formats, with no single record of who applied, when, or for which cohort.

No visibility

Once a jury member said they'd "take a look," there was no way to see if they had, or what they thought, until someone asked directly.

Manual tallying

Combining scores across jurors meant manually averaging numbers pulled from separate emails or sheets before a decision could even be discussed.

The system

One system of record, four roles, no new infrastructure.

01

Applicant

Signs in with a Google account or a passwordless one-time email code, submits once through a guided wizard, and tracks status from the same place.

02

Jury

Sees only what's explicitly assigned, scores a fixed six-criterion rubric, and leaves a recommendation - nothing more to configure.

03

Staff

Owns the pipeline end to end: opens cohorts, assigns jurors, watches scores land, and records the final decision.

04

Admin

Everything Staff can do, plus user roles, portal branding, and the full audit trail of every action taken.

Runs on Google Sheets + Apps Script - the infrastructure CTIE already trusts and already has access to. No separate database, server, or hosting bill to maintain.

The journey

From submission to decision, every step is tracked.

1
Cohort open
Applicant completes the wizard
Self info, startup info, product & revenue, financials & stage, business plan, and supporting documents. An Application ID is issued the moment they submit.
2
Intake review
Staff assigns jury members
Each assigned juror is notified by email and gets viewer access to the application's uploaded documents.
3
Jury scoring
Six criteria, 1-5 each, plus a recommendation
The first score submitted automatically moves the application from Submitted to Under Review - no manual status update needed.
✓
Final call
Staff records the decision
Shortlisted, Selected, Not Selected, or On Hold. A Shortlisted decision automatically books a Calendar review meeting with a Meet link for the applicant and every assigned juror - and the applicant is emailed at every single status change, automatically.
Scoring, standardized

The same six criteria, every application, every juror.

Each criterion is scored 1 (weak) to 5 (excellent). The average across all six becomes that juror's total; the average across every assigned juror becomes the application's aggregate score.

Built for four audiences

Not one dashboard trying to be everything.

01

Applicant

  • One place to apply and track - no separate email thread to keep alive.
  • Edit until reviewed - fix mistakes any time before a decision locks it.
  • Notified automatically - every status change triggers an email.
02

Jury

  • Only what's assigned to you - no wading through the full pipeline.
  • One consistent rubric - same six criteria for every application.
  • Revisable - update your score any time before a decision is made.
03

Staff

  • Full pipeline visibility - scoring progress and aggregate scores at a glance.
  • One-click decisions - meeting scheduling and applicant emails happen automatically.
  • Cohort control - open and close application windows on your own schedule.
04

Admin

  • User management - add or deactivate Staff, Jury, and Admin access.
  • Branding control - swap the portal's logos without touching code.
  • Full audit trail - every submission, assignment, and decision, timestamped.
Security, briefly

Access is checked on every call - not just at sign-in.

Role re-derived server-side

Every privileged action looks up the caller's role fresh from the Users sheet on every call. The client never gets to assert who it is.

Passwordless, rate-limited

The one-time email code expires in 10 minutes, and sign-in locks for 15 minutes after 5 incorrect attempts.

Nothing fails silently

Branding, emails, and the application form are all built to degrade gracefully - a missing logo or a flaky mail client never blocks the actual workflow.
